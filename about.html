The Story

How BraapTrax
Started

The short version: I dislocated my shoulder for the fourth time, finally booked the surgery, and spent the recovery getting bored out of my mind on the couch.

I'd always wanted to get outside more, and now I had adult money and an itch I couldn't scratch from the sofa. So I bought a dirt bike — mostly on impulse, partly because skiing in the off-season wasn't cutting it.

One ride turned into ten. Ten turned into a crew. The sport got under my skin in a way nothing else has. It's now my favorite thing I do.

Strava wasn't built for the dirt. So I built something that was.

I kept reaching for Strava and finding the same gaps — no maintenance log, no real way to rate trail difficulty for two-wheel, no way to track who I rode with and what bike I was on. The numbers were there. The context wasn't.

So I started building. BraapTrax is the tool I wanted: GPS rides, a real garage with service intervals, trail notes that mean something to dirt riders, and a crew layer that respects how we actually ride together.
